Thomas Sankara Tribute Page
Thomas Isidore Noël Sankara

Here are the Main Events in Revolutionist Sankara's Life:

  • 1949 - 21st December born in Yako, French Upper Volta as the third of ten children to Joseph and Marguerite Sankara.
  • 1966 - enteres the military academy of Kadiogo in Ouagadougou with the academy's first intake at the age of 17.
  • 1970 - at 20 years of age, goes to military academy of Antsirabe, Madagascar to further his military studies.
  • 1973 - graduates as a junior officer from the military academy of Antsirabe.
  • 1974 - earned fame for his heroic performance in the border war between Upper Volta and Mali, but years later he renounced that the war was as "useless and unjust", due to the his growing political consciousness.
  • 1976 - becomes commander of the Commando Training Center in Po. Also met Blaise Campaorë in Morocco.
  • 1981 - September, appointed Minister of Information in Saye Zerbo's military government, where he his mode of operations seemed odd to other officials: he biked to work instead of driving a car, encouraged investigative journalism instead of censoring journalists and newspapers; this led to publications of government scandals by both privately-owned and state-owned newspapers.
  • 1982 - 12th April, resigns in opposition to what he saw as the regime's anti-labour drift, declaring "Misfortune to those who gag the people!"
  • 1983 - becomes prime minister in January but is dismissed just after four months (17th) May after pushing Ouédraogo's regime for more progressive reforms. He is then arrested after the French President's African affairs adviser, Guy Penne, met with Col. Yorian Somé.
  • 1983 - 4th August, becomes president at the age of 33 after a coup d'état organised by his friend Blaise Compaoré. The coup was supported by Libya, which by that time was on the verge of war with France in Chad.
  • 1984 - on the first anniversary of his accession, renames the country Burkina Faso meaning "the land of the upright poeple" in Moré and Dyula, the two major languages of the country. Also gives a new flag and writes the country's new national anthem.
  • 1986 - Burkina Faso cotton and wheat production increases dramatically and the country obtained self-sufficiency in food production. This was achieved due to his reforms on feudal landlord powers held by tribal chiefs.
  • 1987 - 15th October, he is killed by an armed group with twelve other officials in a coup organized by his former colleague and friend Blaise Compaoré.
